Hassan II Mosque

  • One of the largest mosques in the world, this architectural masterpiece is located on the shores of the Atlantic Ocean. The mosque's minaret is the tallest in the world, and the interior is adorned with stunning craftsmanship.
  • Location: Boulevard Sidi Mohammed Ben Abdallah, Casablanca, Morocco.
  • Significance: The mosque is one of the largest in the world and a major religious and cultural landmark in Casablanca. It was completed in 1993 and is known for its stunning architecture and intricate details.
  • Architecture: The mosque's design combines traditional Islamic architecture with Moorish influences. The minaret stands at 210 meters (689 feet) and includes a laser that points towards Mecca.
  • Visiting Information: The mosque is open to non-Muslim visitors, and guided tours are available.

Corniche

  • Location: The Corniche stretches along the coastline of Casablanca.
  • Description: The Corniche is a picturesque promenade that runs along the waterfront, providing breathtaking views of the Atlantic Ocean. It's a popular spot for both locals and tourists to unwind, take a leisurely stroll, or enjoy outdoor activities.
  • Attractions: Along the Corniche, you'll find a variety of cafes, restaurants, and shops. The area is also known for its gardens and green spaces, making it a pleasant place to escape the city's hustle and bustle.
  • Nightlife: The Corniche comes alive at night, with many beach clubs offering a vibrant nightlife scene.

Ain diab beach

  • Location: Ain Diab, Casablanca, Morocco.
  • Description: Ain Diab Beach is a popular destination along the Corniche, offering a sandy shoreline along the Atlantic Ocean. The beach is known for its vibrant atmosphere, with numerous beach clubs, cafes, and restaurants lining the promenade.
  • Activities: Visitors can relax on the beach, take a dip in the ocean, or engage in water sports such as jet skiing and windsurfing. The beach is especially lively in the evenings when locals and tourists gather to enjoy the sunset.
